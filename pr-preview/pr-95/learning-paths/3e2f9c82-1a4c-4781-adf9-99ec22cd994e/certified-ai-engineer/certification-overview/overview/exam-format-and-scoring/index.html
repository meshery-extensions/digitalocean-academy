Exam Format & Scoring

The structure, domains, weighting, and scoring rules of the DO-CAIE exam.
2 minutes read

The DO-CAIE assessment combines a written exam with a hands-on capstone. You must pass both to earn the credential.

Written exam structure 🔗

  • Format: multiple-choice (single- and multiple-answer) plus short scenario questions.
  • Questions: 60 scored questions.
  • Duration: 90 minutes.
  • Pass mark: 70%.
  • Delivery: online, proctored, taken directly inside the academy.

Domain blueprint 🔗

Every question maps to one of seven domains. The weighting tells you where to focus your study time.

#DomainWeight
1AI-Native Cloud fundamentals & service selection12%
2Foundation models, serverless inference & the model catalog14%
3Building agents with the Gradient AI Platform18%
4Knowledge Bases & RAG14%
5GPU compute, 1-Click Models & model serving14%
6Cloud native AI operations with Meshery & DOKS16%
7Production engineering: routing, security, observability & responsible AI12%

Domains 3 and 6 carry the most weight: building real agents and operating the infrastructure that serves them are the heart of the credential.

Capstone 🔗

The capstone is a practical, end-to-end build (see the Capstone Project Guide course). It is graded against a rubric covering functionality, grounding/RAG quality, infrastructure and Meshery operations, safety (guardrails/evaluations), and documentation. A passing capstone plus a passing written exam earns the credential.

Scoring rules 🔗

  • Each written question is worth a fixed number of marks; multiple-answer questions require all correct options and no incorrect ones for full marks.
  • Scenario questions are auto-graded against expected answers.
  • Your final written score is the percentage of total marks earned. 70% or higher passes.
  • The capstone is pass/fail against the rubric, with reviewer feedback.

What to bring 🔗

For the written exam: nothing but focus — it is closed-book. For the capstone: a DigitalOcean account (trial credit is fine), the doctl CLI, and a Meshery deployment.

The **DigitalOcean Certified AI Engineer (DO-CAIE)** is the capstone credential of the
DigitalOcean Academy. It validates that you can design, build, serve, operate, and govern
production AI applications on DigitalOcean's AI-Native Cloud — and that you can manage the
underlying cloud native infrastructure with [Meshery](https://meshery.io/).

> **This path is the study guide for the credential.** For the official competency blueprint,
> prerequisites, and exam policies, see the
> [DigitalOcean Certified AI Engineer (DO-CAIE) certification](https://cloud.layer5.io/academy/certifications/3e2f9c82-1a4c-4781-adf9-99ec22cd994e/digitalocean-certified-ai-engineer/),
> and take the [DO-CAIE Certification Exam](https://cloud.layer5.io/academy/challenges/3e2f9c82-1a4c-4781-adf9-99ec22cd994e/docaie-certification-exam/) when you are ready.

## Who it is for

- **AI/ML engineers** building agentic and retrieval-augmented applications on the
  [Gradient AI Platform](https://docs.digitalocean.com/products/gradient-ai-platform/).
- **Platform & DevOps engineers** running model-serving workloads on GPU Droplets and
  [DigitalOcean Kubernetes (DOKS)](https://docs.digitalocean.com/products/kubernetes/).
- **Backend developers** integrating serverless inference and the Inference Engine into products.

## What it proves

A certified DO-CAIE holder can:

- Choose the right DigitalOcean AI service for a given workload and cost/latency target.
- Call foundation models through serverless inference and the Inference Engine using the
  OpenAI/Anthropic-compatible API.
- Build managed agents with instructions, knowledge bases (RAG), functions, and multi-agent routing.
- Add guardrails and evaluations so agents are safe and measurable.
- Deploy and serve open models on GPU Droplets, 1-Click Models, and DOKS GPU node pools.
- Operate AI infrastructure with Meshery: import clusters, author Designs, performance-test
  inference endpoints, and observe GPU workloads.
- Apply production practices: routing, security, networking, observability, and responsible AI.

## How you earn it

The credential has two parts that must both be passed:

1. A **written exam** (multiple-choice: single- and multiple-answer questions) - pass mark **70%**.
2. A **hands-on capstone** project, graded against a published rubric.

## Recommended preparation

Work through the academy's five core learning paths in order, then this certification track:

| Path | Prepares you for domains |
|------|--------------------------|
| AI Foundations on DigitalOcean | 1, 2 |
| Building Agentic AI with the Gradient AI Platform | 3, 4 |
| GPU Compute & Model Serving | 5 |
| Cloud Native AI Infrastructure with Meshery & DOKS | 6 |
| Production AI Engineering & MLOps | 7 |

No prerequisite certification is required, but comfort with the Linux command line, basic
Kubernetes, and either Python or TypeScript is assumed.

Continue to **Exam Format & Scoring** to learn exactly how the exam is structured.
